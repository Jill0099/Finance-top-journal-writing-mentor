# Prompt 3: Rewrite Abstract

## For JFQA (100–140 words)

```
You are an expert academic editor for top finance journals.

Rewrite my abstract following the JFQA 4-move structure (100–140 words):

Move 1 (1 sentence): [Method/sample] + [research question]
Move 2 (1–2 sentences): Main finding — specific, directional, with magnitude
Move 3 (1 sentence): Robustness — "These results are robust to [alternative measures / fixed effects / subsamples]"
Move 4 (1–2 sentences): Identification OR interpretation — "To establish causality, we employ..." OR "Overall, this evidence is consistent with..."

Rules:
- 100–140 words total
- Include at least one specific number (%, coefficient magnitude)
- No table references
- End with theoretical interpretation, not a method restatement

My details:
- Main finding with number: [e.g., corruption reduces patents by 17.4%]
- Identification: [e.g., IV using ethnic fractionalization and founder's home state corruption]
- Robustness: [e.g., state-year FE, alternative corruption proxies, subsamples]
- Interpretation: [e.g., consistent with corruption reducing social welfare via impeding innovation]

[PASTE YOUR CURRENT ABSTRACT BELOW]
```

## For JCF (130–160 words)

```
You are an expert academic editor for top finance journals.

Rewrite my abstract following the JCF 5-move structure (130–160 words):

Move 1 (1 sentence): Institutional setting / quasi-natural experiment
Move 2–3 (2–3 sentences): Main findings with specific percentages
Move 4 (1 sentence): Mechanism channel
Move 5 (1 sentence): Policy implication — "This study offers novel policy insights by showing..."

Rules:
- 130–160 words
- At least 2 specific percentage figures
- Mention the mechanism explicitly
- End with policy insight or contribution claim
- Do NOT list keywords (they go in the Article Info box separately)

My details:
- Setting/reform: [e.g., 2014 China SOE executive compensation reform]
- Main findings with numbers: [e.g., SOEs reduce abnormal labor investment by 3.91–4.82%]
- Mechanism: [e.g., strengthened internal governance + reduced social comparison]
- Policy insight: [e.g., pay restrictions reduce vertical agency costs in weak institutional environments]

[PASTE YOUR CURRENT ABSTRACT BELOW]
```
